# Chapter 8 — Growth

The ladder, and what it actually means.

Firma Labs doesn't use conventional corporate titles. We have four primary classes, each describing a relationship to the work and the network — not a rung on a hierarchy.

---

| Class | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Advisor** | A trusted counselor or domain expert, contributing judgment, relationships, or specific expertise. Advisors are not employees. They are family. |
| **Fellow** | An entrepreneur in residence or builder on a bounded engagement. Fellows hold significant autonomy on specific projects and are often transitioning toward Partner or toward their own ventures. |
| **Associate** | A full contributor carrying a defined slice of the work. Associates own outcomes within their domain and grow toward Partner as trust and weight accumulate. |
| **Partner** | Someone who carries the vision with weight equivalent to their domain. Partners set direction, hold strategy, and speak with authority both inside and outside the org. |

---

## How growth happens here

Growth at Firma Labs is a function of **trust extended** and **weight carried**, not time served. An Associate who steps up and carries what a Partner normally carries becomes a Partner, full stop. A Partner who wants to go build their own thing and return as a Fellow is honored, not punished.

The fail-at-greatness principle applies here too. Growth trajectories are drawn for where you could go if you swung the hardest swing you believed in, not where you would land if you played safely. We'd rather promote someone who missed a huge target than someone who hit a small one.
