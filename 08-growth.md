# Chapter 8 — Growth

The ladder, and what it actually means.

Firma Labs operates as a class-based structure. Each class describes a relationship to the work and the network — not a rung on a hierarchy. Specific class definitions, salary bands, and progression criteria are being formalized; the descriptions below name the operational classes you'll encounter on the team.

---

| Class | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Associate** | A full contributor carrying a defined slice of the work. Associates own outcomes within their domain and grow toward Partner as trust and weight accumulate. |
| **Partner** | Someone who carries the vision with weight equivalent to their domain. Partners set direction within their lane, hold strategy, and speak with authority both inside and outside the org. |
| **Senior Partner** | A Partner whose weight extends beyond their lane to shape direction across multiple domains. Senior Partners carry vision-level responsibility and are trusted to make calls that affect the whole org. |
| **Governance** | Those holding fiduciary responsibility at the foundation level. Governance is not operational; it is the layer that holds the long-term mission, the assets, and the covenant intact across decades. |

---

## How growth happens here

Growth at Firma Labs is a function of **trust extended** and **weight carried**, not time served. An Associate who steps up and carries what a Partner normally carries becomes a Partner, full stop. A Partner who shapes direction across the org becomes a Senior Partner. The progression is recognized when it's already true, not granted as a reward for waiting.

The fail-at-greatness principle applies here too. Growth trajectories are drawn for where you could go if you swung the hardest swing you believed in, not where you would land if you played safely. We'd rather promote someone who missed a huge target than someone who hit a small one.
