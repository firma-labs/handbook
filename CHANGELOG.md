# Handbook Changelog

## v1.2 — April 2026

This update strips specific compensation mechanics from the public handbook in favor of philosophy + reference to individual agreements. Compensation policy is being formalized with legal counsel; specifics belong in offer letters, not the public handbook.

### Compensation chapter rewritten (Chapter 7)

- **`07-compensation.md`** — All specific benefit listings removed (salary, healthcare, time off, deployment, retirement, connectivity, higher education, agentics training, daily lunch). Trust section removed entirely. Replaced with:
  - Opening paragraph naming that compensation is being formalized with legal counsel; mechanics are in individual offers
  - "How we measure your return" — preserved (philosophy)
  - **New: "Economic rights, not equity, not tokens"** — economic rights as the form of compensation; Fragmints as the on-chain representation of those rights, not the rights themselves
  - "What compensation isn't for" — preserved from v1.1 (Anti-Baal)

**Why:** Public handbook language is referenceable in disputes. Specific benefits and Trust mechanics committed publicly become legal exposure if terms change during legal review. Stripping these in favor of "described in your individual offer" is what mature companies do.

### Growth ladder updated (Chapter 8)

- **`08-growth.md`** — Class names updated to reflect Firma Labs' actual structure:
  - Old ladder: Advisor → Fellow → Associate → Partner
  - New ladder: **Associate → Partner → Senior Partner → Governance**
  - "Senior Partner" replaces the previous "Chief" / C-suite framing — Firma Labs uses Senior Partner internally; Chief titles are external/legal only
  - "Governance" added at the top to acknowledge the foundation/board layer
  - "Advisor" and "Fellow" removed from the public handbook (these may exist as relationship categories but are not ladder rungs)
  - No salary specifics; class definitions describe the role-relationship to the work

### How We Leave (Chapter 11)

- **`11-how-we-leave.md`** — Trust paragraph removed; economic rights/Fragmints framing made consistent with Chapter 7. References individual agreement for all mechanics. Wording clarified that leaving does not end already-vested rights.

### Version bump

- **`README.md`** — Version footer updated from `v1.1` to `v1.2`.
